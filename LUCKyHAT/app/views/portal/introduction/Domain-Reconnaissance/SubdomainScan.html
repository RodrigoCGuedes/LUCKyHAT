Subdomain scanning is an essential process in OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) and security assessments, aiming to identify subdomains related to a target domain. Subdomains often host distinct services or systems, which can sometimes expose vulnerabilities or overlooked entry points.

The primary objectives of Subdomain Scanning include:

  • Discovering Subdomains: Enumerating subdomains linked to the main domain to identify additional services or assets that may be part of the target infrastructure.
  • Understanding the Infrastructure: Mapping subdomains to understand the organization’s online footprint, which can reveal critical insights into its digital presence.
  • Detecting Vulnerabilities: Subdomains may expose services or systems that are less secure, making them potential targets for exploitation.

Effective subdomain scanning allows security teams to map out the complete attack surface, identify potential weaknesses, and act proactively to mitigate risks. Subdomains often provide unique insights into a company's infrastructure that may not be visible through the main domain alone.
