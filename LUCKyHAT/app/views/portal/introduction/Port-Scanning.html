Port Scanning is a fundamental technique in network security assessments, used to identify open ports and the services running on them within a target system. This process is a key step in the OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) and penetration testing phases, allowing analysts to map the attack surface of a network.

The main objectives of Port Scanning include:

  • Identifying Open Ports: Detecting which ports on a target system are open and accessible. Open ports can represent potential entry points for attackers if they are running vulnerable services.
  • Service Detection: Determining the type of service and its version running on the open ports. This information is crucial for identifying known vulnerabilities associated with specific services.
  • Network Topology Mapping: Understanding the layout and configuration of the network by analyzing the ports and services exposed to the internet or within an internal network.
  • Assessing Security Posture: Evaluating the security measures in place by identifying potential weaknesses, such as unnecessary open ports or outdated services that could be exploited.

Port Scanning is essential for discovering vulnerabilities that could be exploited by attackers. By identifying open ports and the services behind them, security professionals can assess the risk level and take necessary actions to secure the network.
